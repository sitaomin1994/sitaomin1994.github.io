Research Directions

My research intersection of data management, AI/ML, and healthcare data science. I proposed serveral data quality improvement algorithms (missing data) for distributed healthcare networks. I also applied generative and preditive AI for statistical modeling for EHR and genomic data. Additionally, I develop privacy-enhancing technologies for healthcare, bioinformatics, and financial applications.

Research Projects & Publications

Stream1: Missing Data Imputation in Distributed Healthcare Networks

Our mission is to address the challenge of missing data imputation in distributed clinical networks. We develop imputation algorithms that enable healthcare institutions to collaboratively handle missing data without directly sharing sensitive patient information. These approaches allow improving data quality in distributed clinical networks for downstream analyses and AI models. We developed a toolkit for distributed missing data imputation called FedImpute.

  • Min, S., et al. Cafe: Improved Federated Data Imputation by Leveraging Missing Data Heterogeneity. IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering
  • Min, S., et al. Exploring the inequitable impact of data missingness on fairness in machine learning, IEEE Intelligent Systems
  • Min, S., et al. FedImpute: A Framework for Distributed Missing Data Imputation Research with Python, Under Review Journal of Statistical Software
  • Min, S., et al. Impact of Missing Data on the Fairness of Decision Support Systems, Under Review Decision Support Systems

Stream2: Generative AI for Bioinformatics and EHR data

We also design solutions to apply generative AI for data generation in genomic data for GWAS studies. We also collaborate with anesthesiologists, developing statistical models to support their clinical research. Currently, I’m also interested in real-world evidence analyses on publicly available datasets, e.g., MIMIC. We are developing tools to collect, process, analyze and benchmark public medical datasets called MedDataKit and we are benchmarking ML/DL based clinical prediction models combined with multimodal information for perioperative surgical data for postoperative complications prediction.

Stream3: Secure AI for Finance Risk Management

We propose a federated XGBoost model for financial crime prediction as the 1st prize solution in US PETs Challenge. We also design solutions for data generation of SNPs data for GWAS studies for privacy preservation.

  • Asif, H., Min, S., Wang, X., & Vaidya, J. US-UK PETs prize challenge: Anomaly detection via privacy-enhanced federated learning. IEEE transactions on privacy. (Best Paper Award 2024)
