Research Interests

My research focuses on healthcare data analysis & data privacy, primarily addressing missing data imputation in distributed networks. I also apply statistical modeling for clinical research and clinical prediction models using EHR data. Additionally, I develop privacy-enhancing technologies for healthcare, bioinformatics, and financial applications.

Missing Data Imputation in Distributed Healthcare Networks

Our mission is to address the challenge of missing data imputation in distributed clinical networks. We develop imputation algorithms that enable healthcare institutions to collaboratively handle missing data without directly sharing sensitive patient information. These approaches allow improving data quality in distributed clinical networks for downstream statistical analyses and predictive tasks.

  • Min, S., et al. Cafe: Improved Federated Data Imputation by Leveraging Missing Data Heterogeneity. IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering. [Link]
  • Min, S., et al. FedImpute: A Framework for Distributed Missing Data Imputation Research with Python, submitted to Journal of Statistical Software, [Link]

  • Min, S., et al. MICE imputation for Multi-center Study via External Knowledge Transfer, work in progress

Federated Learning & Data Privacy in Finance and Bioinformatics
